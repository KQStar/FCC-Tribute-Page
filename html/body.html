Stan Lee

The person behind Marvel

Who is Stan Lee?

Stan Lee [Stanley Martin Lieber] was an American comic book writer, editor, and publisher, and Marvel Comics' lead creative writer for almost 21 years.

Timeline

  • 1922 - Born at Manhattan, NYC
  • 1939 - Graduates from high school and joins WPA Federal Theatre Project
  • 1941 - Made his first debut with "Captain America Foils the Traitor's Revenge" in Captain America Comics #3, where he adopted the pseudonym, Stan Lee. He also co-creates his first superhero, Destroyer. Soon, came up Jack Frost and Father Time.
  • 1942 - Enters the United States Army as a member of the Signal Corps. Later transferred to the Training Film Division, where he worked writing manuals, training films, slogans, and occasionally cartooning.
  • 1947 - Marries Joan Boocock
  • 1963 - Introduction of Avengers and the Amazing Spider-Man by Marvel Comics
  • 1965 - Start of the Bullpen Bulletins, which features news about upcoming Marvel publications, information about Marvel staff members, global events, and Stan's Soapbox
  • 1972 - Assumes the role of publisher
  • 1974 - Receives the Inkpot Award
  • 1981 - Moves to California to develop Marvel TV and movie
  • 1994 - Inducted into Will Eisner Award Hall of Fame
  • 1995 - Inducted into Jack Kirby Hall of Fame
  • 2002 - Receives the Saturn Award
  • 2008 - Receives the NEA's National Medal of Arts
  • 2012 - Wins the Vanguard award [Producers' Guild of America] and Lifetime Achievement award [Visual Effects Studio Awards]
  • 2017 - Given honorary membership of the 2nd Battalion of 3rd U.S. Infantry Regiment out of Joint Base Lewis-McChord at the Emerald City Comic Con [2017] for his prior service
  • 2018 - Dies at Los Angeles, California
